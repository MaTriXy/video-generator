<visual_hook_guidelines>
    Scene 0 is the visual hook -- the scroll-stopper. Only Scene 0 gets this treatment. Design it as a phased animation sequence in your videoDescription.

    <hook_rules>
    **Creative (these are rules, not suggestions):**
    1. The main visual action must begin IMMEDIATELY -- It must describe dramatic motion or transformation. Never describe the hero "sitting still", "hovering", "resting", or "holding" before the action starts. If it contains any stillness, rewrite it.

    **Hero Element:**
    2. One hero element -- not a collage, not a grid, not multiple competing elements. Pick ONE thing and build around it.
    3. Hero must occupy more than 50% of the screen area. Describe it as LARGE and prominent -- never use words like "small", "tiny", "compact", or "minimal" for any element in Scene 0.
    4. Hero must be visible from the very first frame. First frame must NEVER be empty, blank, or just a background.

    **Motion:**
    5. All animations fast and snappy. No slow fades, no lazy drifts.
    6. At least one rapid scale change (zoom, grow, dramatic size shift) early in the scene.
    7. Secondary elements enter staggered (not all at once) to create rhythm.
    8. No element static for more than 1.5 seconds during the hook.

    **Color and Contrast:**
    9. Opening frame must have strong visual energy -- high contrast, vivid colors, and a background that feels alive. Avoid flat, empty, or lifeless backgrounds for Scene 0.

    </hook_rules>

    <hook_mistakes_to_avoid>
    **Do NOT copy example visuals.** Examples show the expected level of detail and phased structure. Your hook must use completely different visual concepts and metaphors specific to the video's topic.
    </hook_mistakes_to_avoid>

</visual_hook_guidelines>
